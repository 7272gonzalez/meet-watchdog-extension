Privacy Policy

Meet Watchdog Chrome Extension — Last updated May 2026

Short version: Meet Watchdog reads your Google Calendar to detect active meetings. All processing happens inside your browser. No data is ever sent to us or any third party.

What the extension accesses

Meet Watchdog requests read-only access to your Google Calendar in order to:

  • Read the titles, start times, and video call links of your calendar events
  • Determine which meetings are currently active or starting soon
  • Check whether you have that meeting open in a Chrome tab

Access is granted through Google's OAuth 2.0 flow via Chrome's built-in identity API. The extension only requests the calendar.readonly scope — it cannot create, edit, or delete calendar events.

How your data is used

Calendar data is used solely to power the meeting alert feature. Specifically:

  • Event titles and start times are used to identify active meetings and compose the alert notification text
  • Video call URLs (Google Meet, Zoom, Teams) are used to open the correct call link when you click Join Now
  • A small record of which meetings have been alerted or attended is stored locally in your browser (chrome.storage.local) so the extension knows when to stop alerting

Data storage and retention

All data stays on your device:

  • No server. Meet Watchdog has no backend server. Nothing is transmitted off your computer.
  • Local storage only. The only things written to storage are your settings (alert timing preferences) and a small state file tracking which meetings you've attended. Both are stored in Chrome's local extension storage on your machine.
  • OAuth token. Your Google access token is managed entirely by Chrome's identity system. Meet Watchdog never reads, stores, or logs the token value itself.
  • Clearing data. You can reset all meeting state at any time via the extension popup → Reset meeting state. Uninstalling the extension removes all stored data.

Data sharing

Meet Watchdog does not share any data with anyone. There are no analytics, no crash reporting services, no advertising networks, and no third-party SDKs. The only external connection the extension makes is directly to Google Calendar API (www.googleapis.com) to read your own calendar — using your own credentials.

Permissions explained

  • identity — to sign in with your Google account via Chrome's built-in OAuth flow
  • tabs — to check whether a meeting URL is already open in a Chrome tab (so it knows you've joined)
  • notifications — to show the meeting alert pop-up
  • alarms — to check your calendar once per minute in the background
  • storage — to save your settings and meeting attendance state locally
  • offscreen — to play the alert sound using the Web Audio API

Children's privacy

Meet Watchdog is a productivity tool intended for adults. It does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.
